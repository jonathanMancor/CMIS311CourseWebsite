Summary

Chapter 3, "Winning Telephone Skills," talks about the skills needed for service providers to effectively communicate with customers over the telephone, and identifies common pitfalls to avoid in call handling. This chapter focuses on the importance of telephone communication in business, given its widespread use in various forms such as simple voicemail and advanced Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) systems.

The chapter emphasizes that every customer interaction over the phone is impacts the success of the organization. Key moments in a typical service call include answering the call, managing the conversation, and closing the call appropriately. It also points out the opportunities to display a positive attitude, which can enhance customer satisfaction.

A major source of customer frustration is being put on hold for long periods or being transferred multiple times. To minimize this frustration, service providers need a solid understanding of the organization's telephone systems and be able to listen to and respect customers' preferences while still managing their expectations.

The chapter suggests improvements of telephone skills through self-study, call monitoring, and customer feedback, such as in the form of satisfaction surveys. Recording calls for self-review or seeking direct feedback from colleagues and supervisors can help in identifying strengths and areas for improvement. Additionally, using scripts for consistency and developing good habits are key points that were touched on to provide superior customer support. The chapter concludes by highlighting the importance of self-care and maintaining a caring, can-do attitude to ensure positive and professional telephonic interactions with customers.

Themes:

  • Customer Service Excellence: Enhancing customer interaction through refined telephone skills.
  • Professional Communication: Maintaining professionalism in voice and demeanor.
  • Technical Proficiency: Understanding and using telephone technology effectively to improve service delivery.

Glossary of Terms

  • Automated Attendant: An Automatic Call Distributor (ACD) feature that routes calls based on the caller’s input on a touch-tone phone.
  • Automatic Number Identification (ANI): Reveals the caller’s phone number, provided by long-distance carriers.
  • Available State: A state in an ACD indicating readiness to take calls.
  • Cold Transfer: Transferring a call after ensuring the other party has successfully connected.
  • Computer Telephony Integration (CTI): Merges computer and telephone systems to enhance information exchange and productivity.
  • Hot Transfer: A call transfer method involving a three-party conference call.
  • Screen Pop: A CTI feature displaying caller information on an analyst’s screen.
  • Skills-Based Routing (SBR): An ACD feature that directs calls based on the match between the caller’s needs and the analyst’s skills.
  • Target Resolution Time: Expected time to resolve a customer incident.
  • Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP): Converts voice to data for transmission over the internet.
  • Voice Response Unit (VRU): Uses integrated technologies for information retrieval or function execution, also known as Interactive Voice Response Unit (IVRU).
  • Warm Transfer: Introducing the caller and recipient during a call transfer but not staying connected.
  • Wrap-Up Mode: Prevents new inbound calls to an analyst immediately after a call to allow for administrative tasks.
