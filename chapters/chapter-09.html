Summary

Chapter 9, "Minimizing Stress and Avoiding Burnout," talks abut the high stress levels commonly associated with customer support roles and provides strategies for service desk analysts to manage stress, time, and workloads to prevent burnout. The chapter talks about how customer service as an inherently stressful profession and highlights the importance for self-management skills.

Stress is described as the body's response to life's demands, and while it can be a motivating force when managed well, excessive or poorly managed stress can lead to both physical and emotional health issues. The chapter encourages you to accurately identify the sources of their stress and to develop coping mechanisms.

Glossary of Terms

  • Eustress: A healthy form of stress that helps keep you motivated and enables you to feel a sense of accomplishment.
  • Institutional stressors: The stressors that accompany the type of business you are in or the state of the organization where you work.
  • Repetitive stress injuries (RSIs): Physical symptoms caused by excessive and repeated use of the hands, wrists, arms, and thumbs, occurring from tasks that use force, repeated strenuous actions, awkward postures, and poorly designed equipment.
  • Situational stressors: The stressors that accompany the type of work you do.
