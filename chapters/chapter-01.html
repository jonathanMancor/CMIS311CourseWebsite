Summary

Chapter 1 of the book discusses delivering high customer satisfaction through effective service desk operations and trends shaping the technical support field. It underscores the critical role of the service desk as the primary contact point in technical support, where customer interactions significantly influence their overall perception of the company and its products. The chapter emphasizes the importance of implementing all available assets such as people, processes, technology, and information to ensure service desk success.

It highlights the growing demand for technical support driven by computing technologies and the resulting career opportunities in customer service and technical support due to a shortage of IT professionals. It also touches on the importance of managing customer expectations with a proactive 'can do' approach and highlights the evolving nature of the support industry, which offers significant opportunities for individuals with business, technical, soft, and self-management skills.

Additionally, it outlines various trends affecting the service desk industry, such as multigenerational and multichannel support, continuous service availability, and the overall professionalization of service desk roles.

Themes:

  • Role Clarity: Understanding the distinct roles within the technical support sector.
  • Skill Development: Mastering a mix of essential skills for effective customer support.
  • Industry Trends: Keeping an eye of current developments in the field and how they influence support strategies.

Glossary of Terms

  • Automatic call distributor (ACD): A telephone technology that routes incoming calls to the next available analyst or to a queue if all are busy.
  • Configuration management system (CMS): A collection of tools and databases for managing IT asset information and linking it to support activities.
  • Customer service value chain: A series of activities that add value in serving customers.
  • Desktop virtualization: The separation of a PC desktop environment from the physical machine using the client/server model.
  • Fee-based support: Support services that customers pay for on a per-use basis.
  • Service provider: A service desk staff member who interacts directly with customers.
  • Incident management system: Technology that offers enhanced trouble ticketing and management reporting capabilities.
  • Infrastructure Library (ITIL): A set of best practices for IT service management.
  • ISO/IEC 20000: An international standard for IT service management.
  • IT service management (ITSM): A discipline focused on the quality of IT services and the relationship between the IT organization and its customers.
  • Knowledge base: A database used by a knowledge management system to store essential data.
  • Knowledge engineer (KBA): Person responsible for the knowledge management process and accuracy of the knowledge base.
  • Knowledge-Centered Support (KCS): A knowledge management strategy developed by the Consortium for Service Innovation.
  • Level zero support: Self-help solutions enabling customers to resolve incidents on their own.
  • Multi-level support model: A service structure that escalates unresolved incidents to higher levels or external experts.
  • Network and system administration: Activities related to user account management, data security, and system maintenance.
  • Network monitoring system: A tool for observing network performance to minimize the impact of incidents.
  • Operational Level Agreement (OLA): An internal agreement to support the achievement of Service Level Agreement (SLA) targets.
  • Peer-to-peer support: Informal support sought from colleagues or other departments.
  • Web 2.0: A web concept that promotes user interaction and content creation, exemplified by blogs and social networks.
