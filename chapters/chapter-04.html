Summary

Chapter 4, "Technical Writing Skills for Support Professionals," talks about the role that modern technologies such as the Internet, email, instant messaging, chat, knowledge management systems, and social media play in Innovating service desk operations. These technologies have expanded the ways in which service desks gather, organize, and deliver information, requiring the integration of technical writing into the skill set of support professionals.

The chapter emphasizes the value of information and the importance of possessing strong writing skills to effectively capture and convey data. Support professionals are often required to engage in various forms of written communication, including logging tickets, sending emails, and crafting messages via instant messaging and chat. Additionally, they need to develop content for FAQs, knowledge base solutions, reports, and even policies and procedures.

Good technical writing is explained in this chapter as having an understanding of the target audience and the document’s purpose. Before writing, it's important to understand the audience’s needs and their intended use of the information. Adhering to documentation standards ensures accuracy and consistency, which are vital for effective communication.

The chapter says that technical writing, like any skill, improves with practice and familiarity with writing rules. It offers tips for developing a clear, effective writing style such as in professional email communication. The chapter says that good writing is increasingly becoming a critical part of success in today's digital workplace.

Themes:

  • Impact of Digital Communication Tools: The use of the Internet, email, instant messaging, chat, and social media in support services.
  • Importance of Technical Writing in IT Support: Improving the ability of support professionals to effectively collect and convey information through improved technical writing skills.
  • Development of Technical Writing Proficiency: Effective technical writing and practical tips for enhancing these skills within IT support roles.

Glossary of Terms

  • Case-based reasoning (CBR): A searching technique that uses everyday language to ask users questions and interpret their answers.
  • Extranet: A website accessed via the Internet, available to the general public but requiring a password for full access.
  • Fuzzy logic: A searching technique that presents all possible solutions that are similar to the search criteria, even with conflicting information.
  • Hypermedia: A storage method storing information in graphical form to access non-linearly via hyperlinks.
  • Integrated ITSM solution: A suite of systems used to manage various IT service management processes, also known as enterprise solutions.
