Summary

Chapter 8, "Teams and Team Players in a Service Desk Setting," is about teamwork in customer support environments, particularly due to the complexity of tasks and the many skills required. The chapter outlines the benefits of working in teams and how to be a team players within the service desk.

The chapter emphasizes that effective teamwork requires a shared sense of purpose, effective leadership, diversity, openness, trust, and constructive relationships with other support groups. An ideal team environment is described as open and pleasant, allowing to develop naturally through the typical stages of team development—forming, storming, norming, and performing.

To transition successfully through these stages, teams must learn to manage conflicts and reach consensus. Being a good team player involves prioritizing the team's needs over personal interests while still maintaining one's individuality. Team members should understand their unique roles, which are defined by their skills, knowledge, experience, and personal style. The chapter explains the different team member styles: challenger, collaborator, communicator, and contributor.

The chapter also goes into the importance of aligning with the service desk’s mission and goals. Each team member must commit to these goals and perform their roles effectively for the team to succeed. Performance measurement is done with tools like automatic call distributors (ACD), email response management systems, and incident management systems, along with techniques like customer satisfaction surveys and performance monitoring.

Themes:

  • Importance of effective teamwork in customer support environments
  • Roles and contributions of service desk team members
  • Impact of personality types and development stages on team dynamics

Glossary of Terms

  • Application of training investments: A comparison of an analyst’s resolution percent and reopen percent before and after attending training.
  • Collaboration technologies: Technologies that enable multiple users to work together on related tasks; also known as groupware.
  • DevOps: A cultural and professional movement that emphasizes communication, collaboration, and integration between software developers and IT Operations professionals.
  • Early life support (ELS): The stage in the service life cycle that occurs at the end of a deployment and before the service is fully accepted into operation (ITIL definition).
  • First contact resolution rate: The percentage of contacts resolved during a customer’s initial contact compared to the total number of contacts received at the service desk for a given period of time.
  • Groupware: See collaboration technologies.
  • Idle state: An ACD state that occurs when an analyst is logged on to the ACD but is not accepting calls.
  • Reopen percent: The percentage of closed incidents that had to be opened back up within a given time period.
  • Time idle: The average length of time an analyst was idle during a given period of time.
  • Wrap-up mode: A feature that prevents the ACD from routing a new inbound call to an analyst’s extension.
